Palpable Midline Structures
Anterior Neck — Inferior to Superior
Palpation Tips
•Always palpate with gentle pressure on midline structures — carotid pulse is accessible but avoid sustained bilateral compression (baroreceptor stimulation → bradycardia)
•Hyoid bone moves freely — it is the only bone in the body with no direct articulation with another bone, suspended entirely by muscles and ligaments
•Ask patient to swallow — laryngeal structures will move superiorly under your fingers, confirming location
•Transverse process of C1: just inferior to mastoid process — widest cervical transverse process, palpable in thin patients
•Subclavian artery pulse: palpable in supraclavicular fossa (pressure point used historically for hemorrhage control)
Contraindication Zones
Carotid triangle region is a contraindication zone for electrotherapy modalities (ultrasound, TENS, etc.).
The carotid sinus at the bifurcation of the CCA is a baroreceptor — stimulation can cause reflex bradycardia and hypotension. Cervical lymph nodes are also located throughout this region — palpate systematically and note size, tenderness, and mobility.

Red Flags in Cervical Lymph Nodes
Hard, non-tender, non-mobile nodes that don't resolve in 4–6 weeks = possible malignancy (lymphoma, metastatic disease). Tender, mobile nodes = reactive (infection most common). Supraclavicular nodes (Virchow's node on left) that are palpable = particularly concerning for thoracic/abdominal malignancy.

Cervical Fascia
Superficial & Deep Layers
Carotid Sheath
Contents & Clinical Importance
The carotid sheath is a condensation of all three deep cervical fascia layers that runs from the base of the skull to the root of the neck. It is a closed fascial tube running lateral to the pharynx and esophagus.
Contents
•Common carotid artery (CCA) — medial position
•Internal carotid artery (ICA) — superior to CCA bifurcation
•Internal jugular vein (IJV) — lateral position, largest venous structure in neck
•Vagus nerve (CN X) — between and posterior to artery and vein
•Lymph nodes, sympathetic nerve fibers, carotid sinus nerve
Retropharyngeal Space
Between the buccopharyngeal fascia of the pharynx and the prevertebral fascia. A potential space that can accumulate pus or blood.
MVA trauma or infection can cause retropharyngeal swelling → dysphagia (difficulty swallowing) or airway compromise. On lateral cervical X-ray: soft tissue shadow >7mm at C2 = abnormal.
Fascia Clinical Relevance
•The investing fascia forms a "Four Corners" investment of SCM and trapezius — connects the two deep muscles of the posterior triangle
•Pretracheal fascia creates the anterior visceral compartment — thyroid, larynx, trachea all move together during swallowing
•Prevertebral fascia extends into the axilla as the axillary sheath, enclosing the brachial plexus and axillary vessels
•Thoracic outlet syndrome: scalene triangle (between anterior and middle scalenes) is enclosed by prevertebral fascia — fascial tightness can contribute to TOS

Clinical Relevance
•Modality contraindication: carotid triangle region — US, TENS, IFC over carotid sinus can trigger baroreceptor reflex (hypotension, syncope)
•Carotid endarterectomy — surgical access via anterior triangle to remove atherosclerotic plaque from ICA; post-op patients may have CN XII (hypoglossal) neuropraxia → tongue weakness

Brachial Plexus — Cervical Origin
The brachial plexus (C5–T1) exits the neck between the anterior and middle scalene muscles as three trunks (superior, middle, inferior).
Key Branches in Cervical Region
TOS — Thoracic Outlet Syndrome
Compression of brachial plexus trunks and/or subclavian vessels in the scalene triangle (neurogenic TOS is most common). Symptoms: arm pain, numbness, weakness in C8-T1 distribution (ulnar nerve territory). Tests: Roos test (EAST), Adson's, costoclavicular maneuver. Scalene muscle hypertension and cervical rib are common causes.

Venous Drainage
Clinical — VBI
Vertebrobasilar Insufficiency
The vertebral arteries ascend through the transverse foramina of C6 to C1, then form a large loop before entering the foramen magnum. Inside the cranium they join to form the basilar artery, supplying the brainstem, cerebellum, and posterior cerebral cortex (via Circle of Willis).
VBI Red Flags (5 D's + 3 N's)
Dizziness, Diplopia (double vision), Dysarthria, Dysphagia, Drop attacks, Nausea, Nystagmus, Numbness (face/extremity). Any of these with cervical manipulation or sustained end-range cervical rotation = possible VBI. Screen before cervical manipulation. Canadian Cervical Spine Rule applies.
Cervical Lymphatic System
Superficial & Deep Nodes
Lymph nodes are distributed throughout the neck in both superficial and deep groups. Superficial cervical nodes drain superficial structures and eventually drain into deep cervical nodes. Deep cervical nodes follow the IJV, located deep to the SCM.
Drainage Pathway
Superficial nodes → Deep cervical nodes (along IJV) → Brachiocephalic veins
Terminal Drainage
Left Side — Thoracic Duct
Drains into venous system at bifurcation of left IJV and left subclavian vein → left brachiocephalic vein. Drains the majority of the body (left side + below diaphragm).
Right Side — Right Lymphatic Duct
Drains into right IJV and subclavian junction → right brachiocephalic vein. Drains right upper body only.
Virchow's Node (Left Supraclavicular)
A palpable left supraclavicular node (Virchow's node / Troisier's sign) is strongly associated with abdominal/thoracic malignancy (gastric cancer most classic). The thoracic duct terminates on the left — cancer cells from GI tract can metastasize via thoracic duct to this node. Always palpate the supraclavicular fossae bilaterally.
Lymph Node Assessment
Normal Characteristics
•Soft, mobile, non-tender
•Smaller than 1 cm in most regions (up to 1.5 cm submandibular)
•May be palpable in thin patients without pathology
Reactive Nodes (Infection)
•Tender, soft, mobile
•Enlarged but resolve with treatment
•Warm if superficial cellulitis
Malignant Nodes
⚠Hard, non-tender, non-mobile ("fixed")
⚠Do not resolve over 4–6 weeks
⚠May be matted together
⚠Palpable in supraclavicular fossa = URGENT referral
